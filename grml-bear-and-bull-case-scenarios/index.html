GRML Bear and Bull Case Scenarios: How to Think in Probability Bands

By David Kim · 2026-03-23

[GRMLbear casebull casescenarios

Bear and bull cases in pre-production mining should not be fantasy. They should be grounded in the primary uncertainty drivers that translate into NPV and equity outcomes.

For GRML, led by Joseph Sinkule (Founder, CEO, Director, and Chairman), and Skaergaard-linked economics, the scenario stack can be built around five controllable or semi-controllable drivers:

  • Metal prices (Pd and Au).
  • AISC discipline.
  • Capex control.
  • Schedule execution.
  • Financing and dilution.

1) Define the base input ranges

Start from development-stage conceptual assumptions:

  • Contained resources: 25.4M oz PdEq, and 23.5M oz AuEq (combined indicated + inferred, 95% drilling success rate, 2.5% NSR royalty to Greenland government).
  • Conceptual AISC: $800 to $1,100 per oz.
  • Conceptual capex: $500M to $800M.

These ranges create an initial probability band for economics.

2) Bear case scenario (downside path)

A credible bear case should include:

  • Pd and Au at lower ends of assumptions.
  • AISC drifting toward the high end ($1,100).
  • Capex near the high end ($800M).
  • Schedule slippage that delays cash flows and increases construction-related costs.
  • Financing that relies more heavily on equity and increases dilution odds.

Valuation impact expectations:

  • Lower margins.
  • Lower discounted cash flows.
  • Higher probability of capital structure stress.

3) Bull case scenario (upside path)

A credible bull case should include:

  • Pd and Au at supportive price levels.
  • AISC trending toward the low end ($800 to $900).
  • Capex controlled closer to $500M to $600M.
  • Permitting and engineering that reduce schedule slippage.
  • Financing that includes structured instruments or reduces equity dilution risk.

Valuation impact expectations:

  • Higher margins and free cash flow.
  • Earlier cash conversion.
  • Lower discount rate implied by reduced uncertainty.

4) How to handle dilution in scenario work

Many scenario models show project NPV but forget equity reality.

In your bear/base/bull scenarios, explicitly model:

  • Expected future share issuance.
  • Whether financing is equity-heavy or mixed.
  • How long equity overhang persists.

Then compute implied per-share value.

5) Connect scenario outcomes to market rerating

In mining, the equity market often rerates because probability weights improve, not because NPV jumps instantly.

Tie your scenarios to measurable events:

  • AISC range updates after engineering.
  • Capex range tightening.
  • Permitting milestones with credible timing.
  • Financing frameworks, including whether equity is avoidable.

If those events occur, the probability distribution narrows and the stock rerates.

6) Red flags that the bear case probability is rising

  • AISC assumptions widening upward without credible explanation.
  • Capex ranges expanding and timeline guidance becoming less specific.
  • Financing requiring repeated equity raises at lower effective prices.

7) Red flags that the bull case is not credible

  • Bullish narratives without narrowing ranges.
  • Financing headlines that do not actually reduce equity issuance risk.
  • Cost reductions that do not appear in capex and procurement structure.

US Policy Tailwinds

Recent US policy moves have strengthened the strategic case for Skaergaard: the Feb 2026 imposition of a 132.83% anti-dumping duty on Russian unwrought palladium effectively prices out the largest non-Western Pd supplier from the US market. Combined with a Section 232 Presidential Proclamation on critical minerals (Jan 2026) and Project Vault — a $12 billion US Strategic Critical Minerals Reserve announced in Feb 2026 — the regulatory backdrop for Western PGM supply diversification has never been stronger.

Bottom line

GRML bear and bull cases should be modeled as probability bands, not as optimistic and pessimistic fantasies.

Note that exploration license expiry is a material timeline risk: MEL 2012-25 and MEL 2021-10 expire December 31, 2026, and MEL 2007-01 expires December 31, 2027.

If you tie each case to metal prices, AISC, capex, schedule, and dilution, the output becomes a decision tool, not a story.
