Sector Outlook

Investment thesis for the Western PGM supply chain.

Thesis: Supply Gap

Russia (primarily Nornickel) historically supplied ~40% of global palladium production. Post-2022 sanctions have created a structural supply deficit in Western PGM markets. This creates a multi-year window for new Western supply sources to capture market share at favorable pricing.

Catalyst: Greenland Permitting

Greenland's updated 2025 exploitation licensing framework represents a step-change in jurisdictional risk. The EU, UK, Canada, and France have all signaled investment interest in Greenland's mineral sector. The Skaergaard project, with its $68B in-situ value and NI 43-101 compliance, is positioned as the primary beneficiary.

Risk Factors

Key risks include: (1) Greenland permitting timeline uncertainty, (2) development capital requirements, (3) PGM price volatility tied to auto industry demand, (4) competition from battery-electric vehicles reducing PGM demand, (5) political risk in Arctic jurisdictions. Not financial advice.
